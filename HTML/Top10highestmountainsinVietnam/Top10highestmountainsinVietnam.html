Discover the 10 highest mountains in Vietnam

Conquering majestic mountains is the dream and desire of many young people. If your youth is still full of enthusiasm and recklessness, let's Viettrekking Discover and admire the 10 highest peaks in Vietnam.

1. Fansipan 3143m (Lao Cai)

The highest mountain in Vietnam, also known as the roof of Indochina. With a height of 3143m above sea level, Fansipan is a meeting place for many mountain climbers both at home and abroad.
Located about 10km from the town of Sapa, this mountain has many conquering roads but the most choice is still the Ton Tram route (2 days 1 night), then Cat Cat (3 days 2 nights) .
More specifically, although it is the highest mountain, Fansipan is the top mountain that is easy to conquer. So, if you are a beginner to experience trekking, you should choose this peak.

Fansipan is the highest peak in Indochina

3. Putaleng 3049m (Lai Chau)

Vietnam's third highest mountain is 3049m height. The conquering route is often selected by this mountain from Ho Thau commune, Tam Duong district of Lai Chau city. And the ending point is located in Ta Leng commune, Tam Duong district, Lai Chau. Moss and lichen are almost closed to the ancient trunks to the rocks. Brilliant and smooth. Through the continuous slope, exhausted people … And through all levels of emotions throughout the journey.

4. Bach moc Luong Tu – Ky Quan San 3046m

The fourth highest mountain of Vietnam at 3046m high, is the natural boundary of Lao Cai and Lai Chau provinces.
This is a mountain whose name is mistaken for another mountain, but due to its popularity, it is difficult to correct its name. Therefore, the name Bach Moc Luong Tu has been used as its official name up to this point.
Tên gọi chính xIts location is Ky Quan San with the starting point from Ky Quan San village, Sang Ma Sao commune, Bat Xat district, Lao Cai. This pinnacle is now a place “hot” no less than Fansipan, as it is one of the most beautiful cloud viewing spots in the high mountains of northern Vietnam. It can be said “dawn in the clouds” has become the trademark of this Bach Moc mountain.

5. Phan Lien San – Khang Su Van 3012m (Lai Chau)

NThe fifth highest mountain in Vietnam with an altitude of 3012m. The journey to conquer lies on the Vietnamese border route – Trung and has passed landmark number 79, which is also the highest landmark of Vietnam (altitude 2880m).
This peak is located in Phong Tho, Lai Chau and is also one of the mountains that has become the most controversial topic due to its name. Its first popular name was Khang Su Van (possibly because it was confused with Khang Su Van pass?), then it was corrected to be Phan Lien San, and up to the present time the official name. Its yet to be announced.
However, this peak has now been crowned with stainless steel with the name Khang Su Van (of a climbing group) and it will probably become the official name for this high mountain if there is still no correction. from experts in the climbing community.

6. Ta Lien Son 2996m (Lai Chau)

The top of the mountain has another name, Co Trau, perhaps because from the village looking up, the mountain peak is so tall and proud as the back of the buffalo that lives in the primeval forest at the foot of the mountain, so Ta Lien Son.
The recorded altitude on Ta Lien peak is 2996m and becomes the 6th highest mountain of Vietnam, belonging to Ta Leng commune, Tam Duong district, Lai Chau.
Ta Lien forest with diverse primeval vegetation will surprise you. The luxuriant old trees, the large trunk covered with moss and ferns make everything seem like a fairy-tale, mysterious scene. Covering a carpet of white camellia flowers, bright red maple leaves and a carpet of green moss alluring a fresh color. The creeping trunks clinging to the stone look like the tentacles of a strange animal, ready to reach out and swallow the mountain climber looking for the forest.
From above you can see the city quite clearly. Lai Chau town is small but beautiful in the middle of four majestic mountains.

7. Ta Chi Nhu – Pu Luong 2979m (Yen Bai)

  • Height:2979m
  • Place: Xa Ho village, Tram Tau district, Yen Bai
  • Topographic: bare hill, short road is a bit steep
  • Moving date: 2 days 1 night
  • Ideal time: from November to April next year
  • 2 -way length: ~22km
  • Level of difficult: 3 – Advanced

The peak of the lead is the seventh highest mountain of Vietnam with a height of 2979m, located in the area of Xa Ho, Tram Tau and Yen Bai. This mountain is one of the ideal cloud hunting places, also known as “Thiên đường mây nơi hạ giới”. However, at a time when it was not the hunting season for clouds, this mountain was also known as “The Kingdom of Sun and Wind” because the terrain ch is mostly bare hill, so there is almost no place to avoid the sun. You can also see a lot of livestock such as horses or goats raised a lot by ethnic people here. This mountain has the exact name of Phu Luong (According to the US military map in 1975, it is PHU LUONG), but the name Ta Chi Nhu was so popular with the mountain climbing community that it is now used as a name. Official name for this mountain peak.

8. Po Ma Lung 2967m (Lai Chau)

  • Height: 2967m
  • Place: Lang village, Ban Lang commune, Phong Tho district, Lai Chau province
  • Terrain: Long sloping terrain, going along streams and waterfalls, steep slopes
  • Moving date: 3 day 2 nights
  • Ideal travel time: from the end of October to the end of April next year
  • 2 way length: ~44km
  • Level of difficult: 4 – Challenge
This eighth highest mountain of Vietnam is one of the newly discovered mountains not long ago, and its presence changes the list of 10 highest peaks of old Vietnam, with an altitude of 2967m and a Among the mountain peaks, the conquest route is located on the Vietnamese border – Trung, belongs to Phong Tho, Lai Chau. The name of the mountain also has a lot of fun as it makes the confusion of Ky Quan San aka Bach Moc Luong Tu.
In addition, Po Ma Lung is also known by another name, Bach Long. This name according to the narration of the villagers here is associated with the legend of a Dragon who dived into a deep lake here and disappeared. Today, this deep lake has been filled by a mass of mountains falling down and creating a very majestic waterfall. And another more special name, the first conquest of this mountain gave it the name Tuong Tuong in memory of singer Tran Lap (google search) of this band. However, at the present time, Po Ma Lung is used as the official name of this peak number 8 in Vietnam.
The source: Viettrekking

9. Nhiu Co San 2965m (Lao Cai)

  • Height:2965m
  • Place:Sang Ma Sao commune, Bat Xat district, Lao Cai province
  • Topographic: steep terrain, going through waterfalls, many forest roads
  • Moving date: 2 days 1 night
  • Ideal travel time: from the end of October to the end of April next year
  • 2 way length: ~24km
  • Level of difficult: 3 – Advanced

Is the ninth highest mountain of Vietnam with an altitude of 2965m, belonging to Nhiu Co San village, Sang Ma Sao commune, Bat Xat district, Lao Cai. This peak is one of the unspoiled places with primeval forests and rich vegetation offering a variety of landscapes. The view is wide with majestic mountains and is well-deserved as one of the mountains to conquer.

In particular, Nhiu Co San is also a very famous place because it is the starting place of the ancient stone road through the maple forest up to 80km long and the ending point is at Sang Ma Pho, Phong Tho, Lai Chau. This ancient stone road, named Pavi, was built by the French because at that time (1927) the O Quy Ho pass route was still not on the map. The construction process was carried out by 50,000 Thai people – Cats of the Northwest Autonomous Region. An extremely arduous journey for 5 years with sweat and blood poured down the Hoang Lien mountains to complete this road.

The source: Viettrekking

10. Lung Cung 2913m (Yen Bai)

  • Height: 2913m
  • Place: version Lung Cung, Nam Co commune, Mu Cang Chai district, Yen Bai province
  • Topographic: walking in the forest, going uphill
  • Moving date: 2 days 1 night
  • Ideal travel time: from the end of September to the end of April next year
  • 2 way length: ~22km
  • Level of difficult: 3 – Advanced

"Closing the book" Top 10 peaks in Vietnam is Lung Cung, with an altitude of 2913m. Possessing diverse terrain, when conquering Lung Cung, you will pass through green grass valleys and dense primeval forests with large rocks covered with wet moss. It will take 2 Days 1 Night to conquer this mountain. However, the journey to conquer Lung Cung is not too difficult and strenuous. This will be an interesting destination for experienced Trekkers.
